Ragnarök

Ragnarök, the end of the worlds, the apocalypse.
While it is unknown when Ragnarök will happen, it is said there will be three years of summer,
followed by three years of winter, the Fimbul winter.
The world serpent, Jörmungandr will rise from the seas and waves will crash against the land.
Surtr, the fire giant, will rise up and bring fire and flame.
The final battle between gods and giants. One where many of the great legends go to die.

Fenrir

Fenrir is the son of the god Loki and takes the form of an enormous wolf,
one that grows to be able to swallow the moon.
Destined to kill and eat the All-Father, Odin,
to then be pierced by Odin's spear, Gungnirr, wielded by his son Vidar.

Jörmungandr

The world serpent.
It is said that the snake was so long it stretched across Midgard and able to bite its own tail.
In the final battle Jörmungandr will fight Thor and in the end get killed,
however, with only nine steps the god of thunder
falls as well, dead from the serpent's venom.

Nidhugg

The dragon that gnaws at Yggdrassil's, the world tree's, roots along with her seven mates.
It's been foretold that after Ragnarök and after Baldr and the humans that survived and whom
finally have started to build a new kingdom - the dragon of dusk will return.

Surtr

The fire giant, the one who wields a blade that glows brighter than the sun and
whom will steep the world in ash and flame.
Frey, the Aesir god, pierces Surtr's eye before being felled by the giant's sword.
Once the world has burned it will plunge into Ginnungagap,
the nothingness that existed before Odin and his brothers created the worlds.

Loki

The god of mischief.
Also the father of many of the creatures depicted in Ragnarök.
Father of both Fenrir and Jörmungandr but also the horse which Odin rides, Sleipnirr,
but also the goddess of the underworld where souls go who did not die fighting - an after-life
for crooks, brigands and cowards - Hel. Which is both the name of the goddess and the underworld.
He will fight alongside his children against the gods after having been punished for causing
the death of the god Baldr and the giant Tjatse.
His freedom is the beginning of the end of Ragnarök.
