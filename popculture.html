Pop Culture

Whole franchices have been made about the norse mythos, mostly in the forms of

vikings or some type of heathenry, and while rather inaccurate it is quite entertaining.

Here are some examples of movies, shows and books one can indulge in regarding Norse ancestry.

Norse Mythology - Book

In Norse Mythology, Gaiman fashions primeval stories into a novelistic arc that begins with the genesis of the legendary

nine worlds; delves into the exploits of the deities, dwarves, and giants; and culminates in Ragnarok, the twilight of

the gods and the rebirth of a new time and people. - The Coy Caterpillar Reads

Beowulf and Grendel - Book

In the book Beowulf and Grendel, which is an old classic that might be difficult for many to parse through,

Beowulf comes to the aid of the Dane king to help him slay the troll Grendel.

The Last Kingdom

As Alfred the Great defends his kingdom from Norse invaders, Uhtred--born a Saxon but raised by Vikings--seeks to claim

his ancestral birthright. - IMDB

Vikings

The adventures of a Ragnar Lothbrok: the greatest hero of his age. The series tells the saga of Ragnar's band of Viking

brothers and his family as he rises to become King of the Viking tribes. As well as being a fearless warrior, Ragnar

embodies the Norse traditions of devotion to the gods: legend has it that he was a direct descendant of Odin, the god of

war and warriors. - IMDB

13th Warrior

A cultured diplomat joins a band of savage warriors in time to meet an even more fearsome enemy in this historical

adventure. In 922 A.D., Ibn Fadlan (Antonio Banderas) is a Muslim emissary from Baghdad en route to meet with the King

of Saqaliba when he is captured by a gang of Vikings. IMDB
